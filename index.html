What are the requirements for IBA course?

The admission requirements for the International Business Administration (IBA) course at Lauder Business School are as follows:

1. General Matriculation Standard:

Applicants must provide a secondary education certificate that meets the general matriculation standard. This includes a variety of international high school diplomas recognized by treaties with Austria. However, diplomas from certain countries might have substantial differences to the Austrian educational system and may require a governmental admission test.

2. Specific Country Acknowledgment:

High school diplomas from the following countries are acknowledged, provided they align with the Austrian secondary education standards: Albania, Andorra, Armenia, Australia, Azerbaijan, Belarus, Belgium, Bosnia-Herzegovina, Bulgaria, Croatia, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Georgia, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Iceland, Ireland, Israel, Italy, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Latvia, Liechtenstein, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Macedonia, Malta, Moldova, Montenegro, Netherlands, New Zealand, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Russia, San Marino, Serbia, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, Tajikistan, Turkey, Ukraine, United Kingdom.

3. IT Skills Requirement:

Applicants should have knowledge of basic concepts and hands-on experience with Windows operating systems and Microsoft Office. Advanced European Computer Driving License (ECDL) skills are also recommended.

4. Mathematics Proficiency:

Knowledge of Mathematics based on the curriculum of an Austrian secondary school is required, particularly for the course "Mathematics for Economics and Business 1" offered in the 2nd semester. Non-mandatory tutoring is available during the first semester to help students meet this requirement.
